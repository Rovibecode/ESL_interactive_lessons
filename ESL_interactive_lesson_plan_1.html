ESL Lesson Plan: A Day in the Life

Mastering Tenses and Pronouns for Taiwanese Students

Target Audience

Kids & Adults

Level (CEFR)

A1-B1

Total Time

60-75 Mins

Core Focus

Verb Tenses

Lesson Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to perform these key skills, enhancing their communicative confidence.

  • ✅Accurately use present, past, and future simple tenses to describe daily activities.
  • ✅Correctly apply subject pronouns (he, she, we, they) in sentences.
  • ✅Engage in communicative activities to describe and compare routines.
  • ✅Expand their vocabulary related to daily routines.

Lesson Time Allocation

The lesson is structured to maximize interactive practice, with the majority of time dedicated to hands-on activities.

Lesson Procedure

10-15 MINS

A. Warm-up: "Mime My Routine"

Students physically engage by miming and guessing daily activities. This introduces core vocabulary and the present tense in a fun, low-pressure way.

Key Vocabulary:

Daily Routines: 日常作息 (rìcháng zuòxī)

Brush teeth: 刷牙 (shuāyá)

Eat breakfast: 吃早餐 (chī zǎocān)

GRAMMAR FOCUS

B. Reviewing the Tenses

A clear breakdown of how to form and use the past, present, and future tenses, establishing the grammatical foundation for the main activities.

Tense Usage Example Sentence
Past Completed actions Yesterday, I ate breakfast.
Present Habits, facts I eat breakfast every day.
Future Plans, predictions Tomorrow, I will eat breakfast.

40-45 MINS

C. Main Activities

Two interactive scenarios that require students to apply their knowledge of tenses and pronouns to solve problems and create stories.

Activity 1: Routine Storyboard Challenge

Groups create a three-day plan (yesterday, today, tomorrow) for a chosen character (he, she, we, they), presenting their story to the class using all three tenses.

Activity 2: Routine Problem Solvers

Students analyze short problem scenarios and propose solutions, requiring them to justify their choices using the correct tenses.

❓ Scenario Example:

"John missed his bus yesterday. What will he do tomorrow to be on time?"

5-10 MINS

D. Wrap-up: One Sentence Summary

Each student creates one final sentence using a tense and pronoun of their choice to quickly review and reinforce the day's lesson.

E. Homework: My Dream Day / My Crazy Day

To apply what they've learned, students will write a short narrative about a 'Dream Day' or 'Crazy Day', describing events in the past, present, and future.

Choose ONE topic:

  • Dream Day: 夢想中的一天 (mèngxiǎng zhōng de yītiān)
  • Crazy Day: 瘋狂的一天 (fēngkuáng de yītiān)
