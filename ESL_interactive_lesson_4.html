ESL Lesson Plan: Amazing Animals and Nature

Exploring Our World's Wonders

Target: Kids & Adults Level: A1-B1 (CEFR) Time: 60-75 Mins Focus: Descriptive Language, Comparatives

I. Lesson Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

  • 🦁 Identify and name a variety of common animals and basic natural features.
  • 🎨 Use descriptive adjectives to characterize animals and natural environments.
  • 🏃‍♀️ Describe actions commonly associated with animals and natural phenomena.
  • 🏡 Identify and name different animal habitats.
  • ✅ Use the simple present tense to state facts about animals and nature.
  • 📍 Apply "There is/There are" to describe the existence and quantity of animals or features.
  • 📊 Integrate frequency adverbs (always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never) from Lesson 3 to describe habitual animal behaviors.
  • 📈 Formulate simple comparative sentences (e.g., "An elephant is bigger than a monkey," "The ocean is deeper than the river").
  • 🗣️ Engage in simple conversations about animals and nature.
  • ✍️ Describe a favorite animal or natural place using newly acquired vocabulary and grammar.
  • 💖 Express preferences for certain animals or natural environments and give simple reasons.

II. Materials

  • Whiteboard or projector
  • Markers/pens
  • Pictures/flashcards of common animals and nature scenes (e.g., jungle, desert, ocean, mountains, forest)
  • Worksheet for Activity 2 and Homework (to be provided by teacher)
  • Optional: Short video clips or animal sounds

III. Lesson Procedure

A. Warm-up: "Guess the Animal/Nature Sound!" (10-15 minutes)

Students will guess animals/nature elements from sounds or actions to introduce basic vocabulary.

Introduction:

"Hello everyone! Today, we're going to explore the amazing world of 'Animals and Nature'!"

大家好！今天我們要探索奇妙的「動物與大自然」世界！

(Dàjiā hǎo! Jīntiān wǒmen yào tànsuǒ qímiào de “dòngwù yǔ dà zìrán” shìjiè!)

Key Vocabulary:

Animals: 動物 (dòngwù)
Nature: 大自然 (dà zìrán)
Lion: 獅子 (shīzi)
Elephant: 大象 (dàxiàng)
Bird: 鳥 (niǎo)
Fish: 魚 (yú)
Forest: 森林 (sēnlín)
River: 河流 (héliú)

B. Main Activities (40-45 minutes)

Interactive activities to practice descriptive language, actions, habitats, and comparisons.

Activity 1: "Describing Our Wild Friends"

Students use adjectives and action verbs to describe various animals from pictures.

Introduce Descriptive Adjectives & Action Verbs:

Adjectives:
  • Big (大的 - dà de)
  • Small (小的 - xiǎo de)
  • Fast (快的 - kuài de)
  • Slow (慢的 - màn de)
  • Colorful (色彩豐富的 - sècǎi fēngfù de)
  • Wild (野生的 - yěshēng de)
  • Gentle (溫和的 - wēnhé de)
Action Verbs:
  • Fly (飛 - fēi)
  • Swim (游泳 - yóuyǒng)
  • Run (跑 - pǎo)
  • Jump (跳 - tiào)
  • Roar (吼叫 - hǒujiào)
  • Eat (吃 - chī)

Examples (using Simple Present):

"Lions are wild animals. They roar loudly." (獅子是野生的動物。牠們大聲吼叫。) (Shīzi shì yěshēng de dòngwù. Tāmen dàshēng hǒujiào.)

"Birds are often colorful. They fly in the sky." (鳥通常色彩豐富。牠們在天空中飛翔。) (Niǎo tōngcháng sècǎi fēngfù. Tāmen zài tiānkōng zhōng fēixiáng.)

Activity: Show animal pictures. Students describe them using adjectives and verbs.

Activity 2: "Where Do They Live? - Habitats & Comparatives"

Students describe habitats, use "there is/are," and compare animals/nature features.

Introduce Habitats:

  • Jungle: (叢林) (cónglín)
  • Ocean: (海洋) (hǎiyáng)
  • Desert: (沙漠) (shāmò)
  • Forest: (森林) (sēnlín)
  • Polar Regions: (極地) (jí dì)

Examples ("There is/are" & Frequency Adverbs):

"There are many trees in the forest. Bears usually live there." (森林裡有很多樹。熊通常住在那裡。) (Sēnlín lǐ yǒu hěnduō shù. Xióng tōngcháng zhù zài nàlǐ.)

"In the ocean, there are many fish. Whales always swim." (在海洋裡，有很多魚。鯨魚總是游泳。) (Zài hǎiyáng lǐ, yǒu hěnduō yú. Jīngyú zǒngshì yóuyǒng.)

Introduce Comparatives:

"An elephant is bigger than a monkey." (大象比猴子大。) (Dàxiàng bǐ hóuzi dà.)

"A cheetah is faster than a lion." (獵豹比獅子快。) (Lièbào bǐ shīzi kuài.)

Activity: Pair work. Students describe habitat pictures, then compare two animals using comparatives and frequency adverbs. Use the worksheet for structured practice.

C. Wrap-up: "My Favorite Animal Profile" (5-10 minutes)

Each student summarizes their favorite animal, its characteristics, habitat, and actions.

Examples:

"My favorite animal is the dolphin. Dolphins are intelligent and fast. They always swim in the ocean." (我最喜歡的動物是海豚。海豚很聰明也很快。牠們總是在海洋裡游泳。) (Wǒ zuì xǐhuān de dòngwù shì hǎitún. Hǎitún hěn cōngmíng yě hěn kuài. Tāmen zǒngshì zài hǎiyáng lǐ yóuyǒng.)

"I like monkeys. They are playful and usually live in the jungle. They jump from tree to tree." (我喜歡猴子。牠們很愛玩，通常住在叢林裡。牠們在樹之間跳躍。) (Wǒ xǐhuān hóuzi. Tāmen hěn ài wán, tōngcháng zhù zài cónglín lǐ. Tāmen zài shù zhī jiān tiàoyuè.)

IV. Homework Assignment: "Nature Explorer's Journal"

Students will describe animals and natural scenes, applying descriptive language, actions, habitats, and comparisons.

Instructions:

"Write 5-7 sentences for the following journal entries:"

1. My Dream Safari/Nature Trip:

Imagine you are on a trip to a natural place (e.g., a forest, a desert, the ocean). Describe 3-4 animals or natural features you see. Mention what they look like, what they do, and where they live. Use frequency adverbs when possible.

Dream Trip: 夢想之旅 (mèngxiǎng zhī lǚ)

2. Animal Comparisons:

Choose two animals (e.g., a monkey and a bird, a fish and a whale). Compare them using comparatives (e.g., 'bigger than', 'faster than'). Describe what they usually do and where they live.

Comparing Animals: 比較動物 (bǐjiào dòngwù)

Remember to use new vocabulary, descriptive adjectives, action verbs, "There is/are," frequency adverbs, and comparatives. Please write your entries on a separate piece of paper or in your notebook."

請將你的日記寫在一張獨立的紙上或筆記本裡。

(Qǐng jiāng nǐ de rìjì xiě zài yī zhāng dúlì de zhǐ shàng huò bǐjìběn lǐ.)
