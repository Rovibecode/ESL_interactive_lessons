ESL Lesson Plan: A Day in the Life

Mastering Tenses and Pronouns for Taiwanese Students

Target: Kids & Adults Level: A1-B1 (CEFR) Time: 60-75 Mins Theme: Daily Routines

I. Lesson Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

  • ✨ Accurately use present, past, and future simple tenses to describe daily activities.
  • ✨ Correctly apply subject pronouns (he, she, we, they) in sentences.
  • ✨ Engage in communicative activities to describe and compare routines, justifying their choices.
  • ✨ Expand their vocabulary related to daily routines.

II. Materials

  • Whiteboard or projector
  • Markers/pens
  • Worksheet for Activity 2 and Homework
  • Optional: Timer, small prizes (for kids)

III. Lesson Procedure

A. Warm-up: "Mime My Routine" (10-15 minutes)

Students will physically engage by miming and guessing daily activities. This introduces core vocabulary and the present tense in a fun, low-pressure way.

Introduction:

"Hello everyone! Today we're going to talk about 'A Day in My Life' or our 'Daily Routines'."

大家好！今天我們要談談「我的一天」或「日常作息」。

(Dàjiā hǎo! Jīntiān wǒmen yào tántán “Wǒ de yītiān” huò “rìcháng zuòxī”.)

Key Vocabulary:

Daily Routines: 日常作息 (rìcháng zuòxī)
A Day in My Life: 我的一天 (wǒ de yītiān)
Brush my teeth: 刷牙 (shuāyá)
Eat breakfast: 吃早餐 (chī zǎocān)
Go to school/work: 去學校/上班 (qù xuéxiào/shàngbān)
Sleep: 睡覺 (shuìjiào)

B. Main Activities (40-45 minutes)

Two interactive scenarios that require students to apply their knowledge of tenses and pronouns to solve problems and create stories.

Reviewing the Tenses

A clear breakdown of how to form and use the past, present, and future tenses, establishing the grammatical foundation for the main activities.

Tense Usage Example Sentence
Past Simple For completed actions. Yesterday, I ate breakfast.
Present Simple For habits, facts. I eat breakfast every day.
Future Simple For plans, predictions. Tomorrow, I will eat breakfast.

Key Phrases for Time:

Every day: 每天 (měitiān)
Yesterday: 昨天 (zuótiān)
Tomorrow: 明天 (míngtiān)
Last night: 昨晚 (zuówǎn)
Next week: 下週 (xià zhōu)

Activity 1: Routine Storyboard Challenge

Groups create a three-day plan (yesterday, today, tomorrow) for a chosen character (he, she, we, they), presenting their story to the class using all three tenses. Students will **list 4-5 different daily activities** relevant to their scenario instead of using flashcards.

Scenario:

"Imagine you are planning a special 'Day Out' for a friend (he/she), or your family (we), or some visitors (they). You need to describe what their day was like yesterday, what it is like today, and what it will be like tomorrow."

Key Vocabulary:

Special Day Out: 特別的一天 (tèbié de yītiān)

Friend: 朋友 (péngyǒu)

Family: 家人 (jiārén)

Visitors: 訪客 (fǎngkè)

Activity 2: Routine Problem Solvers

Students analyze short problem scenarios and propose solutions, requiring them to justify their choices using the correct tenses.

Introduction:

"Sometimes, our routines have problems! Let's solve them using our new grammar."

有時候，我們的日常作息會遇到問題！讓我們用新學的文法來解決它們。

(Yǒu shíhòu, wǒmen de rìcháng zuòxī huì yùdào wèntí! Ràng wǒmen yòng xīn xué de wénfǎ lái jiějué tāmen.)

Key Vocabulary:

Problem: 問題 (wèntí)

Solve: 解決 (jiějué)

Missed the bus: 錯過公車 (cuòguò gōngchē)

On time: 準時 (zhǔnshí)

Tired: 累 (lèi)

Feel better: 感覺好一點 (gǎnjué hǎo yīdiǎn)

C. Wrap-up: "One Sentence Summary" (5-10 minutes)

Each student creates one sentence describing an activity using a chosen tense and pronoun from the lesson to quickly review and reinforce the day's lesson.

IV. Homework Assignment: "My Dream Day / My Crazy Day"

To apply what they've learned, students will write a short narrative about a 'Dream Day' or 'Crazy Day', describing events in the past, present, and future.

Instructions:

"Choose ONE of the following:"

1. My Dream Day:

Describe your perfect day. Use present, past, and future tenses.

  • What do you usually do on a perfect day? (Present Simple)
  • What did you do on your last perfect day? (Past Simple)
  • What will you do on your next perfect day? (Future Simple)

Dream Day: 夢想中的一天 (mèngxiǎng zhōng de yītiān)

2. My Crazy Day:

Describe a very unusual or crazy day you had, or one you imagine having. Use present, past, and future tenses.

  • What is a crazy thing you do sometimes? (Present Simple)
  • What did you do on your craziest day ever? (Past Simple)
  • What will you do if you have a crazy day next week? (Future Simple)

Crazy Day: 瘋狂的一天 (fēngkuáng de yītiān)

Write at least 5-7 sentences. Remember to use 'I' for yourself and try to include 'he,' 'she,' 'we,' or 'they' if you describe someone else in your story.

"Please write your sentences on a separate piece of paper or in your notebook."

請將你的句子寫在一張獨立的紙上或筆記本裡。

(Qǐng jiāng nǐ de jùzi xiě zài yī zhāng dúlì de zhǐ shàng huò bǐjìběn lǐ.)
